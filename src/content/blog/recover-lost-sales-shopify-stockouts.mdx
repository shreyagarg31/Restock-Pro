A stockout is not always bad news — it can mean strong demand. The problem is what happens **after** the shelf is empty. Without a recovery plan, that demand goes to competitors.

## The revenue leak most stores ignore

When a product sells out:

- Paid ads may still send traffic to a dead page
- Email campaigns may link to unavailable SKUs
- Organic shoppers bounce with no follow-up

Industry estimates vary, but a meaningful share of interested visitors will **not return** unless you capture their contact info on the spot.

## Strategy 1: Capture demand on the product page

Add a back-in-stock widget before shoppers leave. A collapsed **notify me** bar keeps the page tidy; the expanded form collects email and optional name.

This is the lowest-friction recovery tactic because intent is at its peak.

## Strategy 2: Send restock emails automatically

Manual "we're back!" campaigns do not scale. Automate emails when Shopify detects that a subscribed product is back in stock.

Effective restock emails include:

- Product name and image
- Direct link to the product page
- Clear unsubscribe link

Restock Pro handles this workflow end-to-end.

## Strategy 3: Use subscriber data for purchasing

Your back-in-stock subscriber list is a **demand forecast**:

- Which variants get the most signups?
- Which colors or sizes sell out fastest?
- How long do customers wait before unsubscribing?

Export CSVs from Restock Pro and share with your buying team. Use **capturable value** on the dashboard as a planning signal for top-requested products.

## Strategy 4: Set expectations on the page

Pair the notify widget with honest copy:

- "Sold out — expected restock in 2 weeks"
- "Join the waitlist for the next drop"

Transparency reduces frustration and increases signup rates.

## What not to do

- **Do not** hide the widget behind a separate landing page
- **Do not** spam non-subscribers when you restock
- **Do not** ignore mobile layout — most signups happen on phones
