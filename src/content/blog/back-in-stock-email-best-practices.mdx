The widget captures the subscriber. The **restock email** closes the sale. Here is how to write and send back-in-stock notifications that feel helpful — not spammy.

## Send immediately when inventory returns

Speed matters. Shoppers who signed up on a sold-out page are often ready to buy **now**. Automate sends when inventory updates — do not batch restock emails for end of week.

Restock Pro triggers on Shopify inventory webhooks so you do not babysit stock levels.

## Subject lines that work

Keep subjects specific and product-led:

- "The [Product Name] is back in stock"
- "Good news — [Product Name] is available again"
- "You asked, we restocked: [Product Name]"

Avoid vague hype ("AMAZING NEWS!!!") — it hurts deliverability and trust.

## Email body essentials

Every restock email should include:

1. **Product name** — match what they subscribed to
2. **Hero image** — same product they wanted
3. **Primary CTA** — "Shop now" linking to the product URL
4. **Unsubscribe link** — required for compliance and trust

Keep copy short. One job: get them back to the product page.

## Compliance and trust

Back-in-stock emails are **transactional-ish** — the customer requested them. Still:

- Include unsubscribe in every send
- Honor opt-outs immediately
- Do not add subscribers to unrelated marketing lists without consent

Restock Pro includes unsubscribe links in every restock notification.

## Timing and inventory edge cases

Watch for:

- **Partial restocks** — only email when the subscribed variant is available
- **Flash sellouts** — email once per restock event, not on every +1 inventory tick
- **Cancelled products** — remove or sunset subscribers for discontinued SKUs

## Measure what matters

Track:

| Metric | Why it matters |
|--------|----------------|
| Open rate | Subject line + sender reputation |
| Click rate | CTA clarity and product relevance |
| Conversion rate | True recovery of lost sales |
| Unsubscribe rate | Signal of over-mailing or wrong audience |
