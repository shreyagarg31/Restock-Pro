A **notify me button** on sold-out product pages is one of the highest-leverage UX patterns in ecommerce. It turns a dead end into a permission-based marketing channel — with the customer's consent.

## Collapsed vs expanded widget states

A good notify-me experience has two states:

### Collapsed (default)

On page load, the widget should be compact — a single row with a bell icon, **"Notify me when available"**, and a chevron. It should feel native to your theme, not like a popup or banner.

This keeps sold-out pages clean while still advertising the option.

### Expanded (on click)

When a shopper clicks, the widget opens into a short form:

- **Back in stock alerts** label
- Heading: "Get notified when it's back"
- Email field (required)
- Name field (optional)
- **Notify Me** submit button
- Trust line: "No spam. Unsubscribe anytime."

Restock Pro follows this exact pattern on Shopify storefronts.

## Where to place the notify me button

Best placements on product pages:

1. **Directly below the price** and sold-out status
2. **Above the disabled Add to cart button**
3. **Inside the buy box** — not in the footer or sidebar

Avoid hiding the widget below reviews or recommendations. Intent is highest at the purchase decision point.

## Theme editor setup (no code)

Restock Pro installs as a theme app block:

1. Shopify admin → **Online Store → Themes → Customize**
2. Navigate to **Products → Default product**
3. Click **Add block** → **Restock Pro**
4. Drag the block into your product information section
5. Save

No CSS files, no script tags, no developer required.

## Mobile considerations

Most Shopify traffic is mobile. Restock Pro's widget:

- Uses touch-friendly tap targets
- Stacks form fields vertically when expanded
- Inherits your theme's base font for consistency

Test on a real phone after enabling the block.

- **Subscribers per sold-out SKU** — signals demand
- **Email-to-purchase rate** on restock sends
- **Time to restock** vs subscriber growth
