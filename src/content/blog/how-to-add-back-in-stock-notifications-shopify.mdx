Sold-out products do not have to mean lost customers. With the right **back-in-stock notification** setup, you can capture emails on the product page and alert shoppers automatically when inventory returns.

## Why Shopify merchants need back-in-stock alerts

Shopify's default sold-out experience ends at a disabled "Sold out" button. That works for transparency, but it does nothing to recover demand. Back-in-stock notifications add a second path: subscribe now, buy later.

Common use cases:

- **Limited drops** that sell out in hours
- **Seasonal SKUs** that restock on a schedule
- **Supplier delays** where inventory is temporarily zero

## Step 1: Install a back-in-stock app

Choose an app built for Shopify's current theme architecture. Restock Pro uses a **Theme App Extension**, so you do not edit Liquid code or inject scripts manually.

Install from the Shopify App Store, then open the app from your admin to complete onboarding.

## Step 2: Enable the Notify Me widget

In your Shopify theme editor:

1. Go to **Online Store → Themes → Customize**
2. Open a **product page** template
3. Add the **Restock Pro** app block where customers see purchase actions
4. Save and preview on a sold-out product

The widget appears as a clean collapsed bar: **"Notify me when available"** with a bell icon. Customers expand it to enter their email.

## Step 3: Test the signup flow

On a sold-out product:

1. Click the notify bar to expand the form
2. Submit a test email address
3. Confirm the success state appears
4. Verify the subscriber shows in your Restock Pro dashboard

## Step 4: Let automation handle restocks

When Shopify inventory increases for a subscribed product, Restock Pro queues a **restock email** automatically. You do not need to export CSVs or send manual campaigns.

## Tips for better conversion

- Place the widget **above or beside** the sold-out buy button
- Use clear copy: "Get notified when it's back"
- Mention unsubscribe in the widget footer — it builds trust
